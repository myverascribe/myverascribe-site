Where Your Records Live

Your data. Your Drive.
Your control.

Most apps in this category keep your records on the company's servers — a company you don't control, holding the most sensitive details of your family life. They can read it, lose it, or be subpoenaed. And if you tried to leave OurFamilyWizard or TalkingParents, you may not be able to get your data off their servers even if you ask.

Most apps keep your records on the company's servers — a company you don't control that can read it, lose it, or be subpoenaed.

Verascribe is built the opposite way: there's no "co-parent side" — no portal for the other parent to log into, and no setting that could leak.

Why that means private by design

The other apps are two-party platforms — both parents typically have accounts. Verascribe has no account on our side at all. Your records are private not because you remembered to mark them private, but because the architecture makes any other state structural — not a setting, not a permission you forgot to flip.

Your records live in two places, both of which you already own:

  • Your Google Sheets workbook

    The durable record, in your own Google Drive.

  • A Google Drive folder the app creates for you

    For evidence files you upload — photos, screenshots, PDFs. Both are yours before, during, and after your license.

  • We cannot see what you write

    We cannot read your evidence files. We cannot share your records — because we don't have them. The only thing the app sends out to validate your license is the bare minimum: your email, your license token, and the workbook ID.

Proof of Keeping

A record kept honestly —
and the evidence to back it.

If anyone ever questions your record, the answer can't be "trust me" — it has to be something you can show: a transparent history of every change, plus the files that prove what happened.

Scroll to explore

The audit trail doesn't work against you. It works for you.

You can edit or delete any entry — it's your record. But every change is logged automatically, with a timestamp and your attribution. And you can attach the proof itself — a screenshot, a photo, a PDF, almost any file up to 10 MB — to the exact log entry it belongs to, kept in your own Google Drive.

Why that protects you

OFW and TalkingParents track timestamps on messages — but only on messages. Verascribe tracks every operation — create, edit, delete — across every category, so the history speaks for itself. And where the other apps mostly limit attachments to messages or expenses and keep them on their servers (capped by your plan), your evidence files live in a Verascribe folder in your own Google Drive — any file type, on any entry, with no storage tier to outgrow. (A spreadsheet can't really do this — Google Sheets only drops a small image into a cell, no PDFs.)

Common Questions

Before you start.

How is this different from OurFamilyWizard?

OurFamilyWizard stores your records on their servers and charges $110–$300 per year. Verascribe stores your records in your own Google account and charges $24.99 for a full year of access. After year one, Verascribe goes read-only — but your records stay accessible forever. OFW gives you two grace logins in a six-month window after subscription lapse, then locks you out.

Will my co-parent see this?

No. Your records are in your own Google account, accessible only to you. The app is yours alone — there's no "co-parent side" to Verascribe at all.

Can I use this on my phone or tablet?

Not yet. Currently desktop and laptop only. Mobile and tablet support is in active development.

What happens after year 1?

Your records stay in your Google account. View, search, and export them forever — no payment needed. You just can't add new entries until you renew.

Is the PDF court-admissible?

The PDF captures every entry with timestamps and your audit trail. Whether a specific document is admissible depends on your jurisdiction — your attorney is the right person to ask. The audit trail's main job is to let you demonstrate that your records have been kept honestly, which is valuable in any setting where authenticity matters.

What if I have multiple children with different co-parents?

Set up a separate workbook for each co-parent. Same app, same license, completely separate records.
