Permissions, in plain English

What you're granting Google
to let the app run.

Verascribe is a Google Workspace app. When you sign in, Google asks you to approve four specific permissions. Each one is checked individually — you see the full list and can review each before you grant.

See, edit, create, and delete only the specific Google Drive files you use with this app

This is the drive.file scope. It lets the app reach the workbook you pick via Google's own file picker, plus files Verascribe creates inside a Verascribe folder it makes in your Drive. Nothing else in your Drive is visible to it.

Connect to an external service

Used for license validation and to confirm the registry domain hasn't been tampered with. The full host list (four domains total) is in the OAuth Disclosure.

Send email as you

Used to send you a license-registration receipt and to route any "Contact Us" support message you write. The app never reads your inbox, your drafts, or any other message.

Allow this application to run when you are not present

Installs a daily maintenance trigger that refreshes metrics and cleans up temporary files once a day. No data leaves your account.

Implicit permission (not a checkbox)

When you sign in with Google, the app also sees your Google email address (the address only — never your inbox or messages). This is part of Google's standard sign-in. The app uses it to register your trial, validate your license, and reach you for support.

What we do not access

  • Any other spreadsheet in your Drive
  • Your Gmail inbox, drafts, or any messages
  • Your Google Calendar
  • Your Google Contacts
  • Your Google Photos
  • Any advertising or tracking identifier

We do not run a database that holds your custody, financial, or evidence records. The only data we receive on our side is what's needed to validate your license — your email, your workbook ID, and your license token. Everything else stays in your Google account.

Why you're seeing "Unverified"

Verascribe is in active Google security review. Until review completes, Google labels every new Workspace app this way. The label is about Google's review queue, not about the app's security or your data. Once Google completes review, the label goes away and is replaced by a Google Workspace Marketplace listing.

What stays true either way

Your records live in your own Google account. Verascribe has no database holding your custody, financial, or evidence records. We cannot see your logs or your evidence files — the architecture doesn't permit it, regardless of where the app sits in Google's queue.

Verascribe follows Google's API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

If you'd rather wait until verification completes, email support@myverascribe.com and we'll let you know the moment the label changes.

System requirements

What you'll need
to run the trial.

  • A personal Google account is best. Personal Gmail accounts (@gmail.com) work without restrictions.

  • Workspace or school accounts may not work. Google Workspace administrators and school IT often block third-party apps by default. If you sign in with a work or school account and see an error like "admin has not allowed access," you have two options: ask your admin to approve Verascribe Guardian for your account, or sign out and use a personal Google account. We recommend the personal-account route — it's faster, and it keeps your custody records out of your employer's or school's audit logs entirely.

  • A desktop or laptop. Verascribe runs on Chrome, Edge, and Firefox. Safari support is in active testing — if you hit a snag, drop us a line. Currently desktop and laptop only; mobile and tablet support coming soon.
